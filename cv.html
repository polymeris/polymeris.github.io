Wivo Dull Boy

Clojure, Clojurescript, RabbitMQ, OpenVPN

IoT data acquisition and aggregation

The distributed and varied nature of Wivo's sensors poses a challenge when it comes to data acquisition. The sites vary wildly in infrastructure reliability making it mandatory to design a very robust system.

Dull Boy was one of the first microservices implemented at Wivo, while getting away from the MVP's monolithic model. While its companion service Hannibal handled customer-provided data, and was as such a more traditional ETL service, Dull Boy's purpose was to serve as the ingestion point of sensor-generated timeseries. The unreliability in site connectivity and power supply made it a necessity to design reproducible, idempotent data pipeline, for which Clojure statelessness was very well suited.

To decouple spikes in incoming requests, RabbitMQ was used as a loading queue, and to assist in customer isolation and security, VPNs where created. The generation of these services was automated with Docker and Docker Compose.

An operational monitoring UI was also created with ClojureScript. This was later spun-off into its own service.

Wivo Chigurh

Flexible business-oriented time-series database

Clojure, Postgres, ElasticSearch

As a very small startup, every customer was of extreme importance for Wivo. Thus, it was necessary to create a flexible platform which could address the particular needs of each one.

Existing time-series databases were focused on operational aspects, relegating important factors in the retail business, so we implemented our own system based on Postgres and ElasticSearch which addressed those requirements.

Postgres serves as the source of truth, while ElasticSearch serves as a store for materialized views of each customer's business metrics. The pipeline that renders these views is fully configurable, enabling the execution of complex queries involving both data acquired from Wivo's on-site sensors and provided by the client. The produced reports ultimately allow for actionable insights on the customer's side.
